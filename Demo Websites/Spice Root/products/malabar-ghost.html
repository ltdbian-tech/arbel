Origin Story

The Malabar Coast produces pepper of a distinctly mineral, oceanic character that is entirely absent from inland varieties. Our peppercorns are harvested from vine clusters at the base of the Western Ghats, where sea air at 80% humidity during the critical ripening window creates the blend's signature wild salinity.

After harvest, berry clusters spend 36 hours in a controlled brine cure using coastal sea salt — a traditional Kerala preservation method that drives the salt deep into the cell walls, intensifying the pepper's natural piperine volatiles before stone-grinding.

How to Use

Culinary Applications.

◈
Seafood Coatings

Press 1 tsp into fish fillets with a light oil coat before searing. The kokum's acidity tenderises fish proteins while the pepper creates an intense crust on contact heat.

◈
Coastal Fish Curry

Use 2 tsp as the foundation spice in coconut-base Kerala curries. Bloomed first in coconut oil, it amplifies the creaminess of the coconut rather than fighting it.

◈
Grilled Vegetables

Toss aubergine, okra, or baby corn in olive oil and 1.5 tsp before charcoal grilling. The curry leaf notes become deeply aromatic over direct flame.
