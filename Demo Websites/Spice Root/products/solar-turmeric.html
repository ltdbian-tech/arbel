Origin Story

Erode — often called the ‘Turmeric City’ — has cultivated Curcuma longa for over 2,000 years. The laterite-rich red soil of the Erode basin, combined with drip irrigation from the Cauvery River, produces rhizomes with curcumin concentrations that regularly test above 7% — nearly double the national average of 3.8%.

Our Reserve grade is allocated from farmers who use no chemical fertiliser and adhere to a strict 9-month growth period before harvest. After washing, rhizomes are sun-dried on elevated racks at 36–40°C for exactly 14 days — a process that gently concentrates volatile oils without degrading the curcuminoids.

How to Use

Culinary Applications.

◈
Golden Milk

Simmer 1 tsp in 250ml milk with black pepper (to activate curcumin absorption) and honey. The high curcumin concentration means half the usual quantity achieves the same therapeutic and flavour impact.

◈
Rice & Grain Dishes

Add 0.5 tsp per cup of rice to the cooking water with a teaspoon of ghee. The 80-mesh grind disperses instantly and the bright golden colour is dramatically more vivid than standard commercial grades.

◈
Wet Marinades

Combine with yoghurt, garlic, and ginger for chicken or paneer. The high curcumin penetrates proteins more deeply than low-grade turmeric, giving a more even colour and anti-inflammatory properties throughout the meat.
