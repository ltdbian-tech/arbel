Origin Story

The Crimson Tikka Master Blend was originally commissioned by a Michelin-starred kitchen in London in 2019 as a house spice for their Indian tasting menu. The brief: build a tikka masala blend from primary sources that would hold its flavour profile through the heat of professional cooking. Dr. Nair’s formulation went through 4,000 test iterations before the ratios locked.

The 12 components are sourced from 8 different origin regions — Kashmiri chilli from Sopore, coriander from Rajasthan, fenugreek from Madhya Pradesh, amchur from Uttar Pradesh, and so on. Each component is roasted, ground, and proportioned individually before blending to ensure that no single volatile compound dominates on the palate. The result is deeply balanced: neither too fiery, nor too sweet, nor too earthy — but fully, richly all three.

How to Use

Culinary Applications.

◈
Traditional Tikka Masala

Use 2 tbsp per 500g of marinated chicken. Bloom in ghee on high heat until fragrant, then add tomato puree and cream. The Kashmiri chilli and paprika give a vivid, natural colour without artificial colouring — a key differentiator for professional kitchens.

◈
Restaurant Curry Base

Blend into your base gravy at a ratio of 1.5 tbsp per litre of sauce. The 12-component matrix is calibrated to hold its aromatic complexity through prolonged simmering — ideal for batch-cooking in high-volume service without flavour fade.

◈
Grilled Meats & Kebabs

Combine 1.5 tbsp with full-fat yoghurt, mustard oil, and lemon juice for a 12-hour marinade. The amchur and kasoori methi tenderise proteins while the Kashmiri chilli caramelises on the grill to produce the restaurant-grade char finish.
