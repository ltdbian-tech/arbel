Origin Story

Sikkim’s high-altitude cardamom is a Geographical Indication product — legally, there is only one Sikkim Large Cardamom, and it grows nowhere else on earth. Our partner farms sit above 1,600 metres in the Teesta Valley, where monsoon humidity and cool mountain temperatures produce pods with volatile oil concentrations measured at 8.4% — the highest we have ever tested.

The plants are shade-grown under alder trees (locally called ‘Alnus nepalensis’) which double as nitrogen-fixing organisms for the soil. No synthetic pesticides are used or needed. Each pod is hand-snipped at the precise point of ripeness, when the outer husks have turned from vivid green to a deep jade — a skill that takes decades to develop.

How to Use

Culinary Applications.

◈
Desserts & Pastries

Crack two pods and steep in warm cream for 20 minutes before straining for kheer, panna cotta, or crème brûlée. The result is dramatically more complex than ground cardamom: floral, cool, and lasting without bitterness.

◈
Chai & Beverages

Bruise 3–4 pods with a rolling pin and add to your chai at the start of the simmer. The volatile oils bloom into the milk in a way that pre-ground spice never achieves — an aromatic intensity that fills the room.

◈
Rice Pilafs

Add 2–3 whole pods to ghee before toasting your rice for biryani or pulao. The complex citrus-floral notes permeate each grain and provide a natural aromatic counterpoint to heavier warm spices like clove or star anise.
